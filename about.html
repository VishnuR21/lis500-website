Our perspectives on technology and responsibility.

We’re both interested in how technology shapes people’s lives in visibly and invisibly. The bias test and readings helped both of us notice how fast unconscious associations can show up and why intersectionality matters when evaluating technology’s impact.

Vishnu

Intro: I’m a junior at UW–Madison majoring in Computer Science, Data Science, and Information Science. I’m interested in how technology shapes everyday life, especially how algorithms and data systems affect real people. I took LIS 500 to better understand the social impact of the systems I help build.

Background & interests: Outside of class I like building side projects, playing guitar, going to the gym, reading, and cooking. As someone who wants to work in tech long term, I think it’s important to not just know how to code, but also to think about power, bias, and responsibility.

Learning goals for LIS 500: Understand how bias shows up in data systems, connect course ideas to real-world tech impacts, and learn how to design more responsible tools.

Favorite tech & inspiration: I’m fascinated by modern AI tools and their power to generate text and automate tasks. A tech figure who inspires me is Tim Berners‑Lee for his idea that the web should be open and accessible to everyone.

IAT reflection: I took the race IAT. It felt like a fast-paced sorting game and made me think about how quickly our brains make associations, even when we are not fully aware of them.

Intersectionality: People’s experiences are shaped by overlapping identities (race, gender, class, and more). The readings and IAT helped me see that bias isn’t just about one category at a time.

Kavya

Intro: I’m studying Neurobiology and Information Science. I took LIS 500 because I’m interested in how technology affects people in ways we don’t always notice right away.

Background & interests: I like learning across different areas and thinking about how systems are designed, who they’re built for, and who might be unintentionally left out. Outside of class, I spend a lot of time walking, doing Pilates, reading, watching long-running TV shows, and singing, which helps me balance how busy school can feel.

Learning goals for LIS 500: Notice hidden impacts of technology, understand how bias shows up in systems, and connect course ideas to design decisions.

IAT reflection: Taking the bias test felt uncomfortable but eye-opening. It highlighted how quickly our brains make associations without conscious awareness.

Intersectionality: People experience bias and privilege through overlapping identities. The test and readings reinforced that these layers matter, especially in technology.

Tech & impact reflection: Small design choices can shape access and fairness. This experience made me more intentional about questioning systems rather than accepting them as neutral.
