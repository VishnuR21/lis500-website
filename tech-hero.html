Our Tech Heroes

Each of us chose a different tech hero and connected their work to equity, access, and intersectionality in tech.

Vishnu’s Tech Hero: Tim Berners‑Lee

Tim Berners‑Lee invented the World Wide Web and made core web technologies like HTML and HTTP free for anyone to use. That decision lowered barriers for schools, small organizations, and individuals to publish and participate online.

Intersectionality shows up here because access to information is not evenly distributed. Open standards make it easier for communities with fewer resources to build tools, publish stories, and be represented online.

Kavya’s Tech Hero: Angie Jones

Angie Jones is the Vice President of Engineering, AI Tools & Enablement at Block, Inc. She is an award‑winning educator, an international keynote speaker, and a Master Inventor with 27 patented inventions. She also teaches and advocates for better pathways into tech education and testing.

Her work reflects intersectionality by pushing for broader access to learning and by mentoring new technologists. She has highlighted the importance of bringing more women and minorities into tech and has supported programs like Black Girls Code.
