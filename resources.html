Implicit Bias Test (IBT/IAT)

Definition
A timed sorting task that measures automatic associations between concepts.

The IAT is a widely used online test that measures reaction times to reveal possible implicit associations. It is not a diagnosis but rather used as a reflection tool to spark learning and conversation.

  • Project Implicit IAT Test

Intersectionality

Definition: Intersectionality describes how identities overlap (race, gender, class, ability, and more) and shape lived experience.

Intersectionality describes how race, gender, class, ability, and other identities overlap to shape lived experience. It helps us understand why a single-axis view of identity can miss how discrimination actually works.

Everyday examples

Implicit bias can show up in subtle ways such as who gets called on in class, whose names are mispronounced, or how people interpret professional behavior. These moments add up and shape belonging.

In libraries and information spaces, bias can influence subject headings, search results, and collection decisions. Small design choices can create big access gaps.
